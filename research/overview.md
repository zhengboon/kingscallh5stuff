# Overview: Kings and Legends / Rise of Mythos / King's Call

## Summary

This document covers the full history and identity of a long-running browser-based collectible card game that has operated under at least three major English names across multiple publishers and eras. The core game IP appears to originate from a Chinese developer (ChangYou / Changyou.com), was published internationally in multiple licensed variants starting in 2012-2013, and continues to be operated as of 2024-2025 under the name **King's Call** (stylized "Kings Call") on Steam and mobile.

## Developer and Publisher Chain

### Original Developer: ChangYou.com (Changyou)
- **Changyou.com** is a Chinese online game company originally spun off from Sohu.com in 2007. It went public in 2009.
- ChangYou established **GameFuse** in October 2012 as its Western-facing online gaming platform, operated by **ChangYou.com (US) LLC**, based in Redwood City, California.
- GameFuse launched Rise of Mythos on July 2, 2013, also making it available on the GameFuse portal and Facebook.
- GameFuse's portfolio consisted primarily of Rise of Mythos and Wartune.
- As of available records, GameFuse had approximately 79 employees (2024 data) and has not raised external funding.

### GameSpree (Kings and Legends — European/Global)
- **GameSpree** was a Berlin-based publisher of browser and mobile games, backed by gamigo AG.
- GameSpree published the Western / European version of the game under the name **Kings and Legends**, with developer credited as "Just A Game" in some listings.
- GameSpree was acquired by **gamigo AG** on April 28, 2015 (effective retroactively February 1, 2015), for an undisclosed sum paid entirely in new gamigo shares. GameSpree's annual revenue was approximately €2.5 million.
- With the acquisition, gamigo inherited Kings and Legends and War 2 Glory.
- Kings and Legends (European/GameSpree version) continued to operate under gamigo for several more years.
- The gamigo-operated Kings and Legends was officially shut down on **February 9, 2021**. The announcement cited "various reasons" and thanked volunteer community staff.

### Kings and Legends SEA (Southeast Asia Version)
- A separate SEA-licensed version of the game was operated at ftcgames.asia / kalftcgames.asia.
- This version was shut down on **June 1, 2014**, with top-up functions ending May 22, 2014, due to "license renewal issues."
- The Facebook page (KingAndLegend) documented multiple server openings including "Chimera" (launched November 2014), suggesting operations continued in some form past the June 2014 date, or the referenced shutdown was an early server.

### GameFuse / Rise of Mythos
- Rise of Mythos (GameFuse) was available on Kongregate, Armor Games, Facebook, and the GameFuse portal directly.
- The game was shut down on **August 30, 2019**, citing "internal business strategy adjustment and the termination of their partnership with the developers."
- An earlier closure was announced for May 27, 2019, but the actual shutdown was delayed to August 30.
- Rise of Mythos had its own separate mobile app (Android package: `com.gamefuse.riseofmythos.google` and `com.huoys.wzzh.us`) with APK available from approximately 2013-2016.

### 101XP (Russian Version)
- **101XP** (a Russian publisher) also published Rise of Mythos for Russian and Eastern European markets. 101XP is known for publishing games on VKontakte, Odnoklassniki, and other platforms.
- 101XP ran its own servers for Rise of Mythos. The exact shutdown date for the Russian version is not clearly documented.

### Easy Joy Technology Limited (Steam: 王者神域)
- Steam App 1383670, titled **KING GOD DOMAIN** (described as "an innovative WEBGAME"), was released September 1, 2020 by Easy Joy Technology Limited.
- This appears to be an Asian-region version of the same game, playable in English and Simplified Chinese.
- Community discussions on Steam noted this was the Asian Kings and Legends server that had been "region locked to Asia only for the last 10 years" before appearing on Steam.
- Received Mixed reviews (54% positive, 46 reviews at time of research).
- The developer noted "we do not have a good translation team" when releasing English language support.

### Starlight Games / X Star Game Limited (Current: Kings Call)
- **Starlight Games** (Twitter: @starlghtgames) and **X Star Game Limited** co-develop and publish the current Kings Call versions.
- X Star Game Limited's registered address: Rm 12 20/F HO KING COML BLDG 2-16 FA YUEN ST, Mong Kok, Hong Kong.
- Support email: huoyanzhengcheng@gmail.com (and gs@xstargame.com)
- Company website: xstargame.com (hosts Kings Call H5, Kings Call, and Expedition Call)
- **火焰征程 (Flame Journey)**: Released October 25, 2023 (Steam App 2492430), Chinese-language only. Mostly Negative reviews (37% positive, 437 reviews).
- **Kings Call (English)**: Released December 25/26, 2023 (Steam App 2674290). Mixed reviews (64% positive, ~547 reviews).
- Also available on **Google Play** (package: `com.xstar.kingscall`) and **TapTap**.

## Publishers NOT Involved

Research confirmed that the following publishers/companies had **no involvement** in this game series despite being mentioned in some queries:

- **R2Games**: R2Games published a different game, "Summoner's Legion," described as "an exciting combo of Hearthstone and Rise of Mythos" — a competitor game inspired by Rise of Mythos, not the same game.
- **GameSamba**: GameSamba (Everett, WA; offices in Hong Kong/Tokyo) is a publisher focused on licensed games (Star Trek: Alien Domain, anime games). No connection to Kings and Legends / Rise of Mythos / Kings Call found.

## Key Notes on the "Same Game" Question

Community consensus and developer admissions confirm that all versions are functionally the same game:
- All versions use the same core mechanics (4-lane grid combat, same 4 classes, same 7+ factions).
- Kings Call Steam reviews explicitly identify it as the same game as Rise of Mythos / Kings and Legends: "it was a flash game original name rise of mythos."
- From the Kings Call Steam discussion forum: a developer confirmed that old Rise of Mythos accounts could not be recovered in Kings Call.
- The key differences across versions are: localization language, publisher, event schedules, card release history, and power creep from years of operation.
- One Steam reviewer noted Kings Call is "an exact copy of the original it was based off of, minus the localization and years of power creep that the original suffered from."

## Game Genre and Setting

- **Genre**: Browser-based / H5 Collectible Card Game (CCG) with MMO elements; sometimes described as a CCG + tower defense hybrid
- **Setting**: Fantasy medieval world centered on "Silver Heron Ridge"
- **Player role**: Lord of Silver Heron Ridge, protecting the kingdom from invading forces
- **Lore**: The kingdom is under threat from goblins, undead, and mysterious evil forces. A legend exists of an immortal unicorn king enshrouded in fire, sealed in a volcano 1000 years ago by a noble hero.
