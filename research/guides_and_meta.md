# Guides, Meta, Tier Lists, Codes, and Best Cards — Kings Call / Rise of Mythos

> This file compiles all beginner guides, deck building strategies, tier/meta information, gift codes, VIP cost breakdowns, and community tips. Covers both the current Kings Call era and the legacy Rise of Mythos era (mechanics are largely identical).

---

## 1. No Official Tier List Exists

As of April 2026 research, **no formal community-published tier list for Kings Call exists**. The reasons are:

1. The game has a small community (250–380 average Steam concurrent players)
2. Meta is heavily influenced by P2W spending tier — the "best deck" is whoever has the most Awakened/Godlike cards, not a sophisticated rock-paper-scissors balance
3. Community attention is focused on monetization complaints more than strategic optimization
4. Card drop rates are hidden, making it hard to even catalog what cards exist in which packs

**What we know about the meta from community discussion:**
- Awakened cards are categorically superior; the effective top tier is pay-gated
- Mirrorcraft (mage card that copies opponent's elite creatures) is widely considered broken and dominant in PvP
- Ranger class dominates PvP rankings historically (documented across both Rise of Mythos NamuWiki and Kings Call community)
- Godlike pets (from the September 2024 pet system) add another P2W layer to PvP balance
- "NA1 is whale central" — community observation that the oldest servers have the most spending concentration

## 2. Beginner Guide — Getting Started

### 2a. Class Selection (Most Important Early Decision)

At character creation, choose one of 4 classes. **This is permanent** (class change not documented as possible). Each class has exclusive Skill Cards that only they can use.

**Classes and Their Identities:**

| Class | Playstyle | PvP Strength | PvE Strength | Recommended For |
|-------|-----------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Warrior | Tank/buff, strengthen front line | Good | Good | Beginners who like straightforward play |
| Ranger | Control, poison, debuffs, direct damage | **Best** (top PvP rankings) | Good boss killer | Experienced players wanting to dominate PvP |
| Mage | Burst AoE damage, crowd control, Fireball/Lightning | Very Strong (2nd in PvP) | **Best** (boss kills) | Players who want spell-heavy play |
| Priest | Healing, defense, protect elite creatures | Viable | Moderate | Players building expensive Elite/King tier cards |

**Community advice on class selection:**
- Ranger is historically the strongest PvP class: "boasting quick card draw skills, fast effective removal skills, and direct hero damage skills"
- Mage has the strongest boss fight performance
- Rangers are "very powerful against Priests" because Deadly Blow bypasses Sanctuary
- Warriors are good for beginners due to straightforward playstyle
- Priests have strong synergy with Rush decks using high-HP elites

### 2b. Early Game Priority List (F2P)

1. **Complete the tutorial** — free cards and Silver are given as rewards; don't skip
2. **Complete daily quests every day** — daily quests are the primary F2P Silver income
3. **Join an active guild immediately** — guild donation gives guild EXP and personal contribution points; joining a guild also unlocks guild quests
4. **Don't waste Gold** — the daily Activity system rewards you for completing tasks; avoid using Gold just to tick off Activity points when cheaper methods exist
5. **Level 20 target** — Level 20 unlocks the Elite Pack (available for Silver at 15,000 Silver) and the Pet System
6. **Level 25 target** — Level 25 unlocks the Ascension Tower (free daily climb, Tower Coins earned)
7. **Level 35 target** — Level 35 unlocks the Auction House (or VIP status unlocks it earlier)
8. **Link your H5 account to email early** — if playing H5 version, link email immediately to prevent data loss

### 2c. Currency Management

**Silver (free currency):**
- Earn from: Daily quests, battle victories, selling unwanted cards
- Spend on: Standard Pack (5,000 Silver), Elite Pack (15,000 Silver, level 20+), card combination attempts in Alchemy Lab
- Do NOT hoard Silver — the Standard and Elite packs are your main card income; spend Silver on packs consistently

**Gold (premium currency, the "good" one):**
- Earn free (small amounts): Winning 50 PvP battles per week = 20 Gold; Challenge Hall bosses have a chance to reward 30 Gold; daily activity chest gives small amounts
- Spend on: Only things you truly need — avoid spending Gold on Daily Activity tasks that can be completed free
- Saving Gold for Master's Packs (198 Gold, VIP 3 required) is more efficient than wasting it on lower-tier packs

**Rubies:**
- Earned only through the Auction House (selling cards to other players)
- Functionally equivalent to Gold for most purchases
- Key: The Auction House is the only F2P path to earning premium currency through skill/trading

**F2P Gold Weekly Income Estimate:**
- 20 Gold from PvP (50+ battles, any format)
- ~30 Gold potential from Challenge Hall (chance-based)
- Small amounts from daily login and activity chests
- **Total: Approximately 50–80 Gold per week for an active F2P player**

**Comparison**: Paying players get 100 Gold for $1. F2P earns that in roughly 1–2 weeks.

### 2d. Deck Building Fundamentals

**Core Rules:**
- Maximum deck size: **30 cards**
- Minimum deck size: **15 cards**
- You begin with limited card slots; the maximum increases by 1 per level up
- Maximum 3 copies of any same-named card regardless of rarity
- Class skill cards can only be used by their class — build your deck around your class skills

**Basic Balanced Deck Recipe** (community-recommended for beginners):
1. 3–5 frontline melee units (absorb hits while your ranged units attack safely)
2. 4–6 ranged attack units (deal damage from safety behind the front line)
3. 2–3 mage/caster units (AoE damage or support)
4. 2–4 class Skill Cards (your class-specific spells)
5. 1–2 "instant cast" cards if available (cards with very low countdown)
6. Fill remainder with solid melee or specialized utility

**The Auto-Pick Feature:**
- Exists in the deck builder; computer selects "most powerful" cards by stats
- **Not recommended** for PvP or difficult PvE — it ignores synergies and proper card roles
- Useful only as a starting point for very new players who don't understand card interactions yet

## 3. Deck Archetypes

### Archetype 1: Spikerush (Aggro Rush)

The fastest aggro strategy. Goal: deal damage to the enemy hero before they can set up a defense.

**Core Concept**: Use high-speed, low-cost cards to rush across the field quickly. Win before the opponent's slow powerful cards can dominate.

**Deck Size**: As few as 17 cards total (notably smaller than typical 25–30 card decks)

**Key Cards**: Human Cavalry units (+2 movement speed), fast Beast units, direct-damage skills

**Class Synergy**: Warriors (Encourage buffs attack) or Priests (Rush deck synergy noted in community)

**Strengths**: Wins before the opponent can use expensive combos; beats slow Godlike-heavy decks in early turns

**Weaknesses**: Fragile to AoE spells (Mage's Fireball/Lightning Storm clears the board); loses to heavy tank builds that absorb the rush

**When to Use**: Campaign PvE speedrunning; PvP against opponents who expect slow control

### Archetype 2: Ranged Glass Cannon

The classic "ranged archers only" deck. Deals maximum damage from distance while minimizing your own exposure.

**Core Concept**: Pack the deck with ranged attack units (primarily Elves, Human Archers). They attack enemies before enemies can attack back due to range advantage.

**Key Cards**: Elf archers, ranged Human units, any card with "Ranged" attack type

**Class Synergy**: Ranger (provides Sunder Armour to reduce enemy defense, compounding ranged damage); Mage (direct damage spells)

**Strengths**: Very high damage output; excellent for campaign PvE where AI opponents are predictable; clears maps fast (good for grinding Silver)

**Weaknesses**: Vulnerable to close-range rush — if an opponent's fast units reach your archers, they die immediately; no staying power

**When to Use**: Campaign farming; early-to-mid game PvP when opponents haven't set up counters

### Archetype 3: Tank/Buff (Ogre/Human Wall)

Pure durability. Stack the highest-HP defensive units and outlast the opponent through regeneration and buff skills.

**Key Races**: Ogres (regeneration, enrage, high HP), Human Templars/Paladins (armor)

**Class Synergy**: Warrior (Protect/Encourage buffs for sustained offense); Priest (Guardian Angel/Sanctuary keeps Ogres alive)

**Strengths**: Extremely hard to break through; strong in Guild Showdown 4v4 where endurance matters; counters rush decks

**Weaknesses**: Slow; vulnerable to Mage AoE (Frost Nova + Lightning Storm ignores HP advantages); P2W Awakened cards from opponents will eventually outscale any F2P tank build

### Archetype 4: Control/Poison (Ranger Specialty)

Attrition strategy: weaken and slow opponents so they can never reach your hero.

**Key Skills**: Ranger's Tactic: Sunder Armour (reduces enemy DEF), Frenzy (kills all enemy creatures with cost ≤2 in a 2x2 area), Deadly Blow

**Key Races**: Goblins (poison counters, low-cost swarms), Undead (Vampires with lifesteal, Ghosts that return after death)

**Class Synergy**: Ranger is the premier control class — essential for this archetype

**Notable combo**: Yumi Swiftshot + Princess Sarya's Withering Aura: when active together, most basic creatures summoned by the opponent die upon entering battle

**Strengths**: Wins long games of attrition; hard to fight through if the opponent lacks removal; Undead self-revival creates huge value

**Weaknesses**: Expensive to build correctly (requires specific Legendary/Godlike cards for maximum effect); slow; loses to heavy aggro before setup

### Archetype 5: Mage Swarm / Spell Burst

Combines mage spells with front-line units to deal burst damage and clear entire lanes.

**Key Skills**: Mage's Fireball (direct damage), Lightning Storm, Petrify (freeze), Frost Nova (slow)

**Key Races**: Front-line mages (Vampire Mage), support Priestesses

**Class Synergy**: Mage class only (Mage's spell cards are critical to this archetype)

**Notable combo**: Use Petrify or Frost Nova to stop enemy advance while Fireball and Lightning Storm deal direct hero/creature damage

**Strengths**: Boss fight dominance (Mage is cited as best for PvE boss content); can win without strong creatures if spell damage is high enough

**Weaknesses**: Relies on specific Mage skill cards that F2P players may struggle to obtain

## 4. Card Rarity Tier and Power Levels

### Standard Rarity Tiers (Low → High)

| Tier | Stars | Color | Where Obtained | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|----------------|-------|
| Common | ★ | White | Novice's Pack, all packs | Weakest; useful as fodder for combination |
| Good | ★★ | Green | Novice's Pack, Standard Pack | Slightly stronger; still largely early-game filler |
| Rare | ★★★ | Blue | Standard Pack (guaranteed 1/pack), Elite Pack | Meaningful power; workable for mid-game |
| Epic | ★★★★ | Purple | Elite Pack (better odds), Master's Pack | Strong cards; F2P ceiling for most players |
| Legendary | ★★★★★ | Orange | Master's Pack, King's Pack | Powerful; primarily paid access; event drops |
| Godlike | ★★★★★★ | Red | King's Pack (VIP 5 required), Hall of Fame (Godlike version) | Top free-to-obtain tier; requires significant spend |
| Awakened | Above Godlike | Special art | Hall of Fame (VIP 12 exclusive), paid events | True endgame tier; community estimates ~$500/card |

**The F2P Ceiling**: Competitive F2P players can realistically reach **Epic tier** through grinding. Legendary and above requires either significant spending or exceptional luck from very expensive packs.

**Awakened Power Level**: Awakened cards have:
- Higher ATK/HP stats than their Godlike counterparts
- Some have reworked abilities (not just stat increases)
- New artwork for Elite and Skill cards
- Same cooldowns as lower-tier equivalents
- Examples from Kings Call Fandom Wiki:
  - Sentinel (Awakened): +18 HP and +3 Armor vs Godlike version
  - Captain (Awakened): +21 HP and +4 Armor vs Godlike version
  - Guard (Awakened): +12 HP and +3 ATK vs Godlike version

## 5. Notable Named Cards and Elite Mechanics

### Princess Sarya (Halfblood / Elf Elite)
- **Stats**: 1/4, very low HP
- **Ability: Withering Aura** — passive debuff that weakens newly-summoned enemy creatures
- **Key Combo**: With Yumi Swiftshot's "Deadly Reflex," most basic opponent creatures die when entering battle
- **Also combos with**: Undead King Bael's "Call of the Undead King"
- **Weakness**: Extremely low HP; needs protection (Sanctuary, Martyr creatures)
- **How to Obtain**: Available from PvP Shop for 1,500 Medals; also appears in Supremacy events
- **On Awakening**: Stat boost + significant increase to Withering Aura's power

### Big Shuck (Outsider)
- **Abilities**: Strike Again (attacks twice) + Dominating (intimidation/control effect)
- **Role**: Reinforce an advantage or break a tie; fearsome combination of double-strike and control
- **On Awakening**: Gains additional ATK
- **Appears in**: Tower of Heaven event mode

### Wisdom Juju (Goblin or similar small race)
- **Lore**: Leader of the Pixies
- **Featured in**: Supremacy event (Supremacy: Wisdom Juju, appearing in November 2024 event rotation)

### Garghael
- **Status**: Mentioned as an elite monster in discussions about Mirrorcraft abuse
- **Notable**: One of the cards that Mirrorcraft can illegitimately create multiple copies of on-field

### Yumi Swiftshot
- **Ability: Deadly Reflex** — triggers death effects when Princess Sarya's Withering Aura is active
- **Synergy combo**: Paired with Princess Sarya for near-total creature shutdown

### The Four Kings (Godlike — King's Pack Exclusives)
- The only Godlike cards available from King's Pack
- King's Pack requires VIP 5 to access
- Very powerful; cornerstone of top-tier P2W decks

### Soul Summoner Cards
- Earned through the Soul Summoner PvE system (grind-based, ~6–8 months for Legendary)
- **Cannot be Awakened** (this is intentional — forces buying card packs for Awakened versions)
- Cannot be used for collection completion rate (don't appear in the guide)
- Can be enhanced to gold-like quality using Fire Rubies, but this is not the same as Awakening

## 6. Card Packs — Detailed Guide

### Basic Packs (No VIP Requirement)

**Novice's Pack**:
- Cost: 1,000 Silver OR 12 Gold
- Contents: 5 Common/Good cards + 1 Good-or-better guaranteed
- Special: In Kings Call, this pack **exclusively contains Human, Elf, Goblin, and Ogre race creatures**, plus Skill cards for all 4 classes
- Use Case: Early game when building starter decks; not worth Silver once you hit level 10+

**Standard Pack**:
- Cost: 5,000 Silver OR 58 Gold
- Contents: 5 Common/Good cards + 1 Rare-or-better guaranteed
- The primary F2P card acquisition method
- The 5,000-Silver standard pack is "the most suitable for drawing cards" (community consensus)
- Use Case: Main grinding pack for F2P players; open many for Rare card accumulation

**Elite Pack**:
- Cost: 15,000 Silver OR 98 Gold
- Requirement: Level 20+ OR VIP 1
- Contents: Better Epic card odds than Standard
- Use Case: Level 20+ players' main Silver pack; upgrade from Standard when available

### Premium Packs (Gold/Rubies Only)

**Master's Pack**:
- Cost: 198 Gold OR Rubies
- Requirement: VIP 3+
- Contents: 5 Rare-or-better + 1 Epic-or-better guaranteed; Legendary possible
- Use Case: First "real" premium pack; a significant spending threshold for occasional spenders

**King's Pack**:
- Cost: 598 Gold OR Rubies
- Requirement: VIP 5+
- Contents: Epic/Legendary focus; Godlike possible (specifically: the 4 Kings are the Godlike cards available here)
- Use Case: High-end spenders wanting top cards

**Race-Specific Packs**:
- Available for: Human, Elf, Halfblood, Undead, Beast, Goblin+Ogre mixed
- Offer race-specific powerful cards not found in main packs
- Help increase Reputation with a specific faction faster
- Include powerful cards unique to each race

**Event Packs**:
- Available during specific events (not always accessible)
- Can contain very powerful new cards
- Festival Pack: Appears as a common event reward in gift codes (mid-tier pack)
- Witch Pack: Appears in Thanksgiving/Halloween event rewards
- Solar Chest / Legendary Chest: Higher-tier event reward containers

**King's Call H5 Pack Note**:
- H5 has "4 basic packs available to all players regardless of VIP"
- Additional VIP-locked packs exist on top of this
- H5's pack pool is modified from Steam (some cards removed/rebalanced)

### VIP Benefits Overview

The VIP system unlocks progressively better content at each tier:
- **VIP 1**: Unlocks Elite Pack (also unlockable by reaching level 20 without paying)
- **VIP 3**: Unlocks Master's Pack (guaranteed Epic+ per pack)
- **VIP 5**: Unlocks King's Pack (the only source of the "4 Kings" Godlike cards in standard packs)
- **VIP 5–10**: Top 32 VIP spenders per season are **invited to post-season tournaments** with exclusive Gold rewards and card prizes
- **VIP 12**: Hall of Fame card design; maximum current benefits

### The F2P Weekly Income vs. VIP Spending

**F2P earns** approximately:
- 60 Gold per week from Ascension Tower
- 15+ Gold per day as minimum guaranteed (from daily activities and challenge hall)
- Total: approximately **50–80+ Gold per week** through active F2P play

**VIP 1 costs $1** and provides 100 Gold in seconds.

This 100× disparity between F2P weekly earning and the cheapest VIP purchase is a key source of community frustration.

## 9. F2P Strategy Guide

### Weekly Must-Do Checklist

**Daily (every day):**
- [ ] Complete all Daily Quests (primary Silver income)
- [ ] Log in for daily check-in reward
- [ ] Attempt Ascension Tower climb (free daily attempt)
- [ ] Do Challenge Hall with guild members (cooperative boss fights, free daily attempts)
- [ ] Spend earned Silver on Standard or Elite Packs (don't hoard Silver)

**Weekly:**
- [ ] Complete 50+ PvP battles (any format) for the 20 Gold reward
- [ ] Participate in Guild donation (contributes to guild skills; earns contribution)
- [ ] Clear all guild quests
- [ ] Collect weekly ranking rewards (if in top 200 of leaderboard, extra Gold and Medals)
- [ ] Use Tower Shop coins from Ascension Tower (convert to cards)

**As Available:**
- [ ] Challenge Hall co-op fights for a chance at 30 Gold and card rewards
- [ ] World Boss participation (if your server has it active) — rewards even if boss not defeated
- [ ] Check Steam News for gift codes with each server maintenance

### Resource Optimization Tips

**Silver Efficiency**:
- Don't use Silver to levy taxes for Activity points unless you're already doing everything else free
- Standard Pack (5,000 Silver) is the best Silver-to-card-value ratio at lower levels
- Elite Pack (15,000 Silver) is better for Epic card odds once you hit level 20

**Gold Efficiency**:
- Save Gold for when Master's Pack becomes available (VIP 3)
- Don't waste Gold on "Daily Activity" tasks that cost Gold when Silver alternatives exist
- 50 PvP battles = 20 free Gold per week — always do this

**Card Management**:
- Extract (disassemble) duplicate low-rarity cards into fusion materials
- Use Fuse (Alchemy Lab) to convert materials into specific cards you need
- Auction House: sell rare duplicates for Rubies; use Rubies to buy specific cards you want

**Guild Selection**:
- Join the most active guild you can find
- Active guilds = more guild quests = more guild skills activated = stat bonuses for everyone
- Guild Showdown participation requires a coordinated team; coordinate elite card choices to avoid duplicate elites (you want each of the 4 team members to cover different elite roles)

## 10. Guild System Guide

### How Guilds Work

**Guild Creation/Joining**:
- Players can create or join guilds
- Guild capacity limits exist (expandable with guild upgrades)
- Guild search by name available in-game

**Guild Economy**:
- **Donation**: Players donate Silver to the guild; this increases guild wealth/experience AND earns the donating player "contribution points"
- Contribution points can be spent in a guild shop (specific items TBD from available data)
- Guild EXP levels up the guild, unlocking:
  - More guild skills (200+ guild skills documented in APK data)
  - Better bonuses for all members

**Guild Skills**:
- Unlocked as the guild levels up
- Provide passive bonuses to all guild members during all game modes
- Examples include: reduced card combination costs, higher alchemy success rates, bonus stats in PvP (not fully documented in available research)

**Guild Quests**:
- Completing guild quests earns Silver, guild EXP, and reputation
- Active guilds have more guild quests available
- Guild quests do NOT cost Gold to complete (unlike some Activity tasks)

### Guild Showdown (4v4 PvP)

The flagship guild competition mode. Once per week (Sundays), the top guilds compete in 4-player teams.

**Format**:
- 4v4: One player from each guild teams up with 3 guildmates, fighting 4 opponents from a rival guild simultaneously
- Each player fights their individual opponent across 4 simultaneous lanes
- "Every week on Sunday, there will be an epic Tournament as the top Guilds face off in teams of 4, with winners taking home massive prizes for their Team and the entire Guild"

**Winning Criteria**: Win more lanes than your opponent across the 4-player team

**Deck Building for Guild Showdown** (from historical Kongregate guide, applicable to Kings Call):
- Aim for a "balanced deck" with: ranged fighters, mages, instant-cast cards, front-line fighters, defensive walls
- Coordinate with your 3 teammates BEFORE the event:
  - Avoid bringing duplicate Elite monsters across the 4-person team
  - Only 1 copy of each Elite should be on the field per lane — having the same Elite in multiple players' decks wastes potential
  - Discuss who brings which Elite before the event
  - "It is good to carry only 1 or 2 of the same elite card type to avoid overlapping by discussing with your allies in advance"

**Rewards**:
- Weekly contributions to Guild EXP
- Personal contribution points
- "Massive prizes" for winning guilds (specific rewards not fully documented in research)

## 12. Ascension Tower Guide

**What It Is**: A solo challenge mode where you fight progressively harder AI opponents floor by floor.

**Access**: Unlocks at hero level 25

**Basic Rules**:
- Free attempt once per day (resets daily)
- Additional attempts via **Tower Tickets** (purchased with Gold, 30 Gold each; also earnable as event/gift code rewards)
- Starting from Level 1 each week (weekly reset)
- Progress as high as you can; record how many floors you cleared

**Floor Count**: 50 floors minimum (may have been expanded in Kings Call; original Rise of Mythos had 50 floors)

**Loot System**:
- After each successful floor, choose from a "Boss-style loot table"
- Prizes increase in value as you progress further up the tower
- Tower Coins earned from successful floor clears

**Tower Shop**:
- Tower Coins are the Ascension Tower currency
- Spent in the Tower Shop for specific cards and items
- Contents include: Flame Bird Fragments, Skill Tomes, Starlight Chests, Legendary Boxes, Silver, and more
- The Tower Shop is a key F2P path to obtain specific cards that might be otherwise unavailable

**Strategy Tips**:
- Manual play (not auto-combat) is essential for higher floors
- Build a specialized deck for the Tower — you're fighting AI with predictable patterns
- Control/defensive decks outperform rush decks in the Tower (you need to handle escalating difficulty)
- Priest with high-HP Elites + healing skills is historically strong in Tower content

## 13. Challenge Hall Guide

**What It Is**: Cooperative PvE boss fights where up to 4 players team up against powerful enemies.

**Access**: Available from early game (exact level unclear; various scenarios unlock at different levels)

**Format**:
- 6+ different scenarios (each with race-specific enemies)
- Each scenario has 2 difficulty levels + Casual difficulty (Shimmering Cave)
- Each battle has 2 stages
- Stage 2 has a small chance of triggering an "Awakened" boss (much harder version using Godlike/Awakened cards)

**Rewards**:
- Experience and Silver from victories
- Reputation points
- Card flips at end of battle: chance to earn Soul Shards, Awakened Fragments, special saddles/mounts
- Small chance of 30 Gold drop per session

**Strategic Notes**:
- Mage class is noted as "best in boss fights" across community sources
- Coordinate with your 3 co-op partners: avoid duplicate Elites in the team (same advice as Guild Showdown)
- The Awakened boss trigger in Stage 2 is uncommon but provides significantly better rewards when it occurs

## 14. World Boss Guide (Queen Sharia)

**What It Is**: A server-wide event where all players on a server collectively attack a powerful boss.

**Schedule**: Two battles per day at 17:00 and 23:00 server time; each battle window is 1 hour

**Access**: Level 20+ required

**Mechanics**:
- Queen Sharia uses one of 3 random decks (each based on a 1.5-era race: Angel, Demon, or Dragon)
- She has 4 states based on time elapsed: Frail (0–5 min), Moderate (5–15 min), Reviving (15–45 min), Furious (45 min–end)
- Boss is defeated when collective server damage reaches **450 total damage**

**Rewards**:
- Rewards given at end regardless of whether Sharia is defeated
- Reward boxes contain either Epic or Legendary card from the featured "Incarnate" set
- **Participation reward** (minimum 1 completed battle): 50 Silver
- **Damage rewards**: All players who deal 10+ damage receive a reward box
- If Sharia is defeated: all reward boxes are doubled
- **Maximum Damage reward**: Player dealing the most damage receives a bonus reward (only given when total server damage reaches 100+)

**Strategy**: Maximize personal damage output during the Frail phase (first 5 minutes, easiest difficulty). Use a glass cannon setup if possible for maximum damage.

## 15. Reputation System

**How It Works**:
- Battle wins using creatures of a specific race earn Reputation with that race
- Reputation earns Reputation Points (RP) per battle based on the races of cards in your deck
- Higher Reputation levels unlock:
  - Increases to Critical Hit and Block rates for that race
  - Access to a **race-specific Reputation Shop** where specific cards can be purchased for Silver at certain RP thresholds

**Reputation Thresholds**:
- Completing regions and "Reputation missions" awards 100,000 RP each for Human, Undead, Elf, and Halfblood factions
- Epic-level cards available in the Reputation Shop at Reputation Level 28 (with the caveat these are mostly support/tribal cards with low versatility for general use)

**Practical Advice**: Focus Reputation grind on the 1–2 races you most frequently use. The stat bonuses from high Reputation are significant in long-term competitive play.

## 16. Card Combination (Alchemy Lab) Tips

### Combination Basics

**Combining cards** (two identical-name cards of same rarity → chance to produce higher rarity):
- Requires: 2+ cards of the same name and same rank
- Adding additional copies of the same card increases success chance
- Costs Silver
- Higher Alchemy Lab level = higher success chance
- **Failed attempts**: Produce fusion materials (not total loss)

**Recommendation**: Save combination attempts for cards you're sure you want to upgrade. Don't combine Rare-tier or below into Epic unless you have sufficient Silver reserves, because the Silver cost at higher tiers is substantial.

### Extraction

- **Extract**: Disassemble unwanted cards into fusion materials
- Use for: Duplicate low-tier cards you'll never play
- Don't Extract: Any card that could potentially be combined up to a useful rarity

### Fusion

- **Fuse**: Use specific fusion material recipes to craft specific target cards
- The APK analysis identified 2,649 compound effects in the game data, suggesting complex fusion recipe systems
- Check the in-game fusion interface for current craftable cards

### Synthesis (Hybridization)

- **Synthesizer**: Fuse two creatures of DIFFERENT races to create a random **Hybrid** creature
- Requirements: Both cards must be same rarity; requires a Synthesize Crystal or Advanced Synthesize Crystal
- Result: A random Hybrid creature (race combination of the two input cards)
- Risk: Random outcome — you can't target a specific Hybrid
- A new Hybrid creature "Hydra" was added in a 2026 patch (specific date not confirmed)

## 18. Meta Summary (Current State, April 2026)

**Top Power Tier** (effectively P2W only):
- Awakened cards (estimated ~$500+ per card to upgrade)
- Hall of Fame Awakened cards (VIP 12 exclusive)
- High-tier Godlike pets (from paid pet packs)

**Accessible High Tier** (heavy spending, $200–$500):
- Godlike cards (King's Pack, VIP 5 required)
- Multiple Legendary cards from Master's Pack grinding

**Competitive Mid Tier** (moderate spending, $50–$200):
- Legendary cards from King's Pack/event packs
- Epic-based decks with strong class skill synergy

**F2P Viable** (no spending):
- Epic-tier cards through Elite Pack (Silver-purchasable)
- Specific powerful cards from Tower Shop, PvP Shop (e.g., Princess Sarya for 1,500 Medals)
- Strong class skills if your class's free skill drops are good

**Dominant PvP Class**: Ranger (historically and per current community reports)

**Most Overpowered Card (Community Reports)**: Mirrorcraft (enables unlimited Elite copies on field, bypassing 1-per-field restriction) — no balance fix as of research date

**Best PvP Deck Archetype (F2P)**: Control/Poison Ranger with Goblin + Undead creatures; use PvP shop to acquire Princess Sarya over time

**Best PvE Deck Archetype**: Mage burst damage for bosses; Tank/Buff with Priest for Ascension Tower sustainability
