# Monetization: Kings and Legends / Rise of Mythos / King's Call

## Overview

All versions of this game share a consistent Chinese free-to-play monetization model characterized by:
- A multi-tier premium currency system
- VIP rank gating of the best content/cards
- Energy systems (daily limits on free pack opening or activity attempts)
- Gacha/loot box card pack mechanics with undisclosed (or poorly disclosed) drop rates
- Strong P2W (pay-to-win) dynamics in competitive PvP at high levels
- Events with time-limited premium cards
- Regular new card releases to trigger spending

**Note on publishers**: Research confirmed that **R2Games** and **GameSamba** had no involvement in this game series. R2Games published a different game called "Summoner's Legion" which was described as "an exciting combo of Hearthstone and Rise of Mythos" — a competitor/clone inspired by the game, not the same game. GameSamba focused on licensed anime/Star Trek titles.

## Currency Systems

### Rise of Mythos (GameFuse version)
Three currencies:
1. **Silver**: Free F2P currency earned through gameplay (quests, battles, selling cards). Used for lower-tier packs, card combination attempts, and various in-game purchases.
2. **Gold**: Premium currency purchasable with real money. Exchange rate: **$1 USD = 40 Gold**. Available in denominations of $5, $10, $20, $50, $100. Very small amounts earnable through daily login rewards.
3. **Rubies**: Secondary premium currency obtained by **selling cards through the Auction House**. Functionally equivalent to Gold for most purchases. Cannot be directly bought; earned by selling other cards.

### Kings and Legends (GameSpree/gamigo version)
Same structure as Rise of Mythos — Silver (free) + Gold (premium) + Rubies (from Auction House sales).

### Kings Call (current version)
Uses a similar premium currency system. Exact currency names not fully confirmed in all research, but the VIP tier cost guide posted on Facebook describes cumulative spending to reach VIP levels (see VIP section below). Support email and developer contacts suggest "gems" or equivalent premium resources tied to real-money purchases.

## VIP System

### Rise of Mythos VIP System
- VIP ranks from 1–10 (exact cap unclear; some sources suggest higher).
- VIP status is unlocked and advanced by **cumulative Gold spending** (not per-purchase).
- VIP 10 reportedly required cumulative spending of "several thousand dollars, give or take" (community estimate).

| VIP Tier | Prerequisite Spending | Notable Unlock               |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| VIP 1    | Low threshold        | Elite Pack access            |
| VIP 3    | Moderate threshold   | Master's Pack (guaranteed Epic+) |
| VIP 5    | Significant          | King's Pack (Godlike possible) |
| VIP 10   | ~Several thousand USD | Highest tier; best benefits  |

### Kings and Legends (GameSpree) VIP System
Same structure; VIP System page exists on the Kings and Legends Fandom wiki. Unlocking higher VIP tiers required ongoing spending.

### Kings Call VIP System
- VIP tiers expanded from VIP 12 to **VIP 13** as of 2024–2025 (first cap increase "in three years since launch" — though Kings Call only launched December 2023; may reference the development/internal cycle).
- VIP 13 announced with a "Sovereign Awakening Crate" containing Black Opal and rare resources for existing VIP 12 players who upgrade.
- VIP 13 described as providing "bespoke elite benefits."
- Steam announcement confirmed the VIP cap expansion (Steam News App 2674290).
- VIP cost guides shared on the game's Facebook community group.

## Card Pack Monetization

### Standard Packs (Free / Low-Cost)
- Novice's Pack: 1,000 Silver or 12 Gold — Common/Good cards only
- Standard Pack: 5,000 Silver or 58 Gold — Common through Rare drops
- Elite Pack: 15,000 Silver or 98 Gold — Better Epic odds; requires level 20 or VIP 1

### Premium Packs (Gold/Ruby Only)
- Master's Pack: 198 Gold/Ruby — Requires VIP 3; guarantees at least 1 Epic; Legendary possible
- King's Pack: 598 Gold/Ruby — Requires VIP 5; focuses Epic/Legendary; Godlike possible
- Mythos Pack: Variable cost — Higher VIP requirement; best overall rates

### Kings Call Pack System
- 4 basic packs available to all players regardless of VIP status
- Additional premium/VIP packs on top of this
- Community complaint: **Packs do not disclose actual card contents**. Players can spend resources on a pack hoping for a specific card only to find the card is not in the pack's possible drops.
- The Kings Call H5 APK analysis identifies **20+ gacha pools** in the game code.

## Kings Call H5 vs Steam Version Differences

Per the official XstarGame FAQ (xstargame.com/news/index/details/id/73.html):
- H5 and Steam versions run on **separate servers with completely separate data** — no shared progress or account
- The H5 version has "selected and rebalanced cards" with "some high-impact designs removed" for a "fresh meta with more balanced, stable, and strategic gameplay"
- Steam is intended for "hardcore content"; H5 focuses on "social features, cross-platform access, and quick play sessions"
- Guest login available on H5 but users advised to link account (email) early to avoid data loss on cache clear

## Event-Based Monetization

- **Time-limited event cards**: New powerful cards released during 2-day windows as real-money exclusive purchases.
- **Free players** can sometimes obtain lower rarity (Epic or below) versions of these cards cheaply in the Auction House but face significant stat disadvantages versus the Legendary/Godlike paid versions.
- Regular seasonal events tied to holidays and in-game lore events, many requiring premium spending to fully participate.
- Kings Call 2024 examples: National Day gift codes (limited free rewards), new server opening events (NA5 opened November 2024).
- **Gift codes**: Occasionally distributed through Steam news posts (e.g., National Day 2024 gift code).

## Pay-to-Win Analysis

### Community Assessment
Multiple independent sources confirm P2W dynamics:

**GameFAQs Review (Rise of Mythos era)**:
- "New card releases are strictly available only for cash, costing hundreds of dollars."
- Specific card example: A card that shuffles your entire graveyard back into your deck (extremely powerful in control mirrors due to the 30-card deck limit) was available only through paying.
- "Make a game, keep it going and get as much money as possible, while alienating the player base and ignoring complaints."

**Steam Reviews (Kings Call)**:
- "You will very quickly fall behind the top players and cannot be competitive at the highest level" without significant spending.
- Premium card packs locked behind paywall make certain cards unavailable to F2P players "regardless of playtime."
- Events "expect players to drop a few hundred dollars to remain competitive."
- Upgrading elite hero cards ("Awakening") costs approximately **$500 per card**.
- NA1 (North America server 1) described as "whale central."

**Steam Forum Discussion (Kings Call)**:
- History of previous versions losing VIP money when servers shut down raised skepticism.
- Allegations of account blocking after gifting attempts.
- Developer response to complaints: "sorry for the bad gaming experience. We will continue to optimize the game." (July 10, 2024)

### P2W Summary
- **Free-to-play ceiling**: Competitive up to mid-tier PvP; unable to reach top ranks against paying players
- **Spending floor for meaningful competitiveness**: Hundreds of USD estimated
- **Maximum spending ceiling**: Thousands of USD for VIP 10+ status in Rise of Mythos; VIP 13 and Awakened card upgrades ($500+ per card) in Kings Call
- **Gacha transparency**: Poor; drop rates not clearly disclosed

## Historical Monetization Incidents

### Account Loss on Shutdown (Rise of Mythos → Kings Call)
- When Kings Call launched, developers confirmed **old Rise of Mythos accounts could not be recovered**.
- Players who had spent significant money on Rise of Mythos had to start over from zero in Kings Call.
- Developer response: "Sorry, we are unable to recover the original account." Contact directed to: huoyanzhengcheng@gmail.com or the Kings Call Discord.
- This pattern repeated across all version transitions — spending in one version does not carry over to the next.

### Multiple Shutdowns, Recurring Model
- Community consensus: The game has been shut down and relaunched multiple times under new names/publishers as a deliberate business model.
- Each relaunch resets player progress, allowing the publisher to monetize a fresh player base.
- Quote from Steam review: "All other versions of this game died at some point, with this version likely to follow soon."
- Quote from another review: "This is at least the third installment of this game."

## Business Model Context

The monetization model fits a well-documented pattern for Chinese-developed F2P web/mobile games:
1. License the game IP to Western publishers (GameSpree, GameFuse, 101XP, etc.)
2. Operate the game with aggressive monetization (VIP tiers, event cards, gacha)
3. Generate revenue for 3–7 years
4. Shut down servers
5. Re-release the same game under a new name / publisher
6. Repeat

The core IP (developed originally by ChangYou, now operated by Starlight Games / X Star Game) has survived through at least 3 major English-language releases and multiple Asian-region releases across ~12 years (2012–2024+).
