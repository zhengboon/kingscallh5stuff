# History Timeline: Kings and Legends / Rise of Mythos / King's Call

## Disclaimer

This timeline is compiled from community sources, gaming press, developer announcements, and wiki documentation. Some dates are approximate. The game has a complex multi-regional history with overlapping versions running simultaneously across different publishers and regions. Not all shutdown/launch dates are precisely confirmed for every regional version.

---

## Pre-History: The Original Chinese Game

The core game IP appears to have originated as a Chinese browser/web game (known in China under various names). ChangYou.com (subsidiary of Sohu.com, later independent) developed the game and licensed it internationally. The Chinese-facing version appears to have operated continuously under Asian publishers, eventually appearing on Steam as:
- 王者神域 (KING GOD DOMAIN) — Steam App 1383670 — September 2020
- 火焰征程 (Flame Journey) — Steam App 2492430 — October 2023

Community notes suggest the Asian-region game was "region locked to Asia only for the last 10 years" before the Steam releases.

---

## 2012 — Kings and Legends Launches (Western/European Version)

**Publisher**: GameSpree (Berlin, Germany) — backed by gamigo AG
**Developer**: "Just A Game" (as credited on MMORPG.com); underlying IP from ChangYou
**Platform**: Browser (kingsandlegends.com); later Chrome Web Store extension

- GameSpree launched **Kings and Legends** for Western/European audiences.
- The game was described as "a free-to-play, trading-card based MMO" where players protect "Silver Heron Ridge."
- Available through the Chrome Web Store (extension ID: dcklmgnboiioeghonbkbbafbifaoiclf).
- The MMORPG.com listing shows a 2013 release date, but some sources suggest GameSpree began the game in late 2012.

**Note on naming**: The GameSpree Wiki lists the description as "the French version" in some community comparisons, though this is disputed. More likely it was the European/global English version.

---

## 2013 — Rise of Mythos Launches (GameFuse / ChangYou Western Version)

**Publisher**: GameFuse (ChangYou.com US LLC, Redwood City, California)
**Founded**: GameFuse established October 2012
**Platform**: Browser — Kongregate, Armor Games, Facebook, GameFuse.com portal

**May 28, 2013**: Kings and Legends Global announced as launching on GameFuse.
**July 2, 2013**: Rise of Mythos released. The GameFuse blog noted "Rise of Mythos — previously known as Kings and Legends Global" — the name was changed "a couple of months" after launch because "drastic changes that occurred during the game's first few months meant the name no longer fit quite right."

**July 11, 2013**: Rise of Mythos became available on the GameFuse Portal and Facebook.

Also in 2013, the Kings and Legends SEA (Southeast Asia) version launched on ftcgames.asia / kalftcgames.asia.

**Kongregate activity**: Community forums on Kongregate show the name change was announced and discussed in 2013. Players who had been playing as "Kings and Legends" were redirected to Rise of Mythos.

**October 2013**: Engadget's "Rise and Shiny" column reviewed Rise of Mythos, finding it "gleefully" engaging despite initial dismissal. Cash shop described as non-aggressive at this stage.

**November 2013**: Early fan blog riseofmythos.wordpress.com posting game resources.

**December 2013**: "Version 1.3 is Here!" — early update milestone (GameFuse patch announcement).

---

## 2014 — Expansion and First Shutdowns

**January 2014**: Kings and Legends (GameSpree European version) major update "The Reckoning" released.

**January 15, 2014**: Rise of Mythos "Angels and Dragons!" update — introducing Angels and Dragon race cards (v1.5 features). ChangYou issued a press release through GamersHell.

**May 2014**: Kings and Legends (European version) announced iOS mobile launch. Android version listed as "coming soon."

**May 22, 2014**: Top-up (payment) function ended for Kings and Legends SEA version.
**June 1, 2014**: **Kings and Legends SEA shut down**. Official reason: "license renewal issues." SEA players had approximately 1 year of service.

**July 24, 2014**: Kongregate and GameStop announced new mobile game titles including Rise of Mythos (Business Wire announcement), indicating active promotion of the mobile version.

**2014 — GameSpree continues**: Halloween and Undead Pirates events documented in the Kings and Legends (GameSpree) version for 2014, indicating active operation.

---

## 2015 — Major Corporate Changes

**February 1, 2015**: gamigo AG acquired GameSpree GmbH (retroactive effective date).
**April 28, 2015**: gamigo acquisition of GameSpree officially announced. Kings and Legends and War 2 Glory came under gamigo control. GameSpree's annual revenue was ~€2.5 million; expected 2015 EBITDA ~€0.8 million.

**August 17, 2015**: Rise of Mythos mobile APK (Google Play package com.gamefuse.riseofmythos.google) updated.

**December 2015**: Rise of Mythos v2.0 feature — **Hybrid creatures / Synthesis system** introduced on non-Chinese servers. Players could now fuse two different-race creatures to create random Hybrid cards.

---

## 2016 — Mobile Expansion, Continued Operations

**January 7, 2016**: Rise of Mythos US mobile version (package com.huoys.wzzh.us) released on Android.

Kings and Legends (now under gamigo) continued to operate with new servers. Documentation of new server "Bastien" opening (gamigo news archive).

101XP's Russian server for Rise of Mythos continued to operate throughout this period.

---

## 2017–2018 — Maintenance Mode

Both Rise of Mythos and Kings and Legends continued operating in what the community described as "maintenance mode" — receiving periodic updates but not major new content. No major documented events found in research for this specific period beyond:
- Regular maintenance patch notes on Kongregate (documented threads exist for multiple months in 2016–2018)
- Ongoing server operations for both versions

---

## 2019 — Rise of Mythos Shutdown

**Early 2019**: GameFuse announced impending closure of Rise of Mythos servers.
**Announced closure date**: May 27, 2019 (official Facebook announcement: "Announcement: Closure of Rise of Mythos scheduled on May 27th, 2019")
- Reason cited: "internal business strategy adjustment and the termination of their partnership with the developers"

**August 30, 2019**: **Rise of Mythos officially shut down.** The actual closure was delayed approximately 3 months from the originally announced date.
- All servers shut down
- Kongregate-hosted version went offline
- Armor Games version went offline (confirmed offline by November 2020, when Flash was ending)
- Facebook/GameFuse portal versions shut down
- Mobile app effectively abandoned

101XP's Russian server for Rise of Mythos also eventually shut down (exact date unclear from research; documented in en.101xp.com news archive).

---

## 2020 — Asian Version Appears on Steam

**September 1, 2020**: **KING GOD DOMAIN (王者神域)** released on Steam (App 1383670) by Easy Joy Technology Limited.
- The community immediately identified this as the Asian Kings and Legends server that "had been region locked to Asia for 10 years."
- Initially Chinese-only; developer added English support, noting "we do not have a good translation team."
- Received Mixed reviews (54% positive, 46 reviews)
- Players criticized aggressive monetization and low gem value

---

## 2021 — Kings and Legends (European) Shutdown

**February 9, 2021**: **Kings and Legends (European/GameSpree/gamigo version) officially shut down.**
- Announcement stated "this decision was the result of various reasons."
- Developers thanked the volunteer community team.
- This marked the end of the last Western-operated version from the classic era.
- The kingsandlegends.com website became inaccessible for the game (though the domain may still resolve).

Around the same time in early-mid 2021, gamigo shut down several other games (Eden Eternal on February 24, 2021; Twin Saga, Defiance on April 29, 2021), suggesting a broader portfolio rationalization.

---

## 2022–2023 — Preparation for New Steam Releases

Activity was quiet for the Western audience. The underlying game continued in Asian markets.

**October 25, 2023**: **火焰征程 (Flame Journey)** released on Steam (App 2492430) by Starlight Games.
- Chinese-language only; no English support
- Same game mechanics; explicitly identified by community as Rise of Mythos / Kings and Legends
- Received **Mostly Negative reviews** (37% positive, 437 reviews) — primarily due to monetization and lack of new content

**December 3, 2023**: Community post in TCG/Strategy Alliance Steam group announces "Kings Call" returning to Steam on December 20th (English version).

---

## 2023 — Kings Call English Launch

**December 20, 2023**: Soft launch / early access (per community announcement)
**December 25–26, 2023**: **Kings Call (English version) officially released on Steam** (App 2674290) by X Star Game Limited / Starlight Games.
- Free to Play
- Initially available on Steam (Windows, Mac, Linux)
- Community immediately recognized the game as Rise of Mythos / Kings and Legends rebranded
- Early reviews: Mixed reception (appreciation for the game returning + concern about P2W history)

---

## 2024 — Kings Call Active Operation

**January 4, 2024**: "Asian Time Zone Game Zone Announcement" — first major Steam news post of 2024.

**March 11, 2024**: Official Discord announced: https://discord.gg/pYSVfSgbv6

**May–June 2024**: Community steam discussions about P2W. Developer acknowledged "sorry for the bad gaming experience. We will continue to optimize the game." (July 10, 2024 response)

**August 2024**: Peak concurrent Steam player count: **508 players** (all-time peak per Steamcharts).

**September 10, 2024**: **Pet system** launched in Kings Call. Also updated novice guide with new monologue content.

**October 15, 2024**: Event update for new servers (NA4, EU4, AS3).

**November 13, 2024**: **NA5 server** opened at 11:00 AM Beijing time.

**2024 (various)**: National Day gift codes, seasonal event announcements, multiple server openings.

---

## 2024–2025 — VIP Cap Expansion

**2024 (exact month unclear)**: Kings Call announced expansion of **VIP cap from VIP 12 to VIP 13** — the first VIP cap increase "in three years since launch." (Note: game launched December 2023, so "three years" may be an error or refer to the underlying game development cycle.) Existing VIP 12 players received a "Sovereign Awakening Crate" with Black Opal and rare resources.

---

## 2025–2026 — Continued Operation

As of April 2026 (research date):
- Kings Call is still actively operating on Steam (App 2674290)
- Current player count: approximately 232–350 concurrent players
- Monthly average peaked August 2024 at 343 average players; declined to ~151 in March 2025 before rebounding to ~219 in April 2025
- December 2025 saw a +70.47% player surge (123.94 average player gain) suggesting a promotional event or content update
- The game continues to release new servers and seasonal events
