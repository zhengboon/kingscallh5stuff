# Servers, Events, Guild System, and PvP — Kings Call

> This file covers the complete server list, event calendar/rotation, guild system details, PvP tournament structure, and all related infrastructure information for Kings Call. Data spans the 2023–2026 era.

---

## 1. Server Structure Overview

Kings Call operates on a **shard-based server system** where each server is a completely separate game world with its own player economy, rankings, guilds, and event schedule.

**Server regions:**
- **NA** (North America) — servers numbered NA1, NA2, NA3...
- **EU** (Europe) — servers numbered EU1, EU2, EU3...
- **AS** (Asia) — servers numbered AS1, AS2, AS3...

**As of 2025–2026**: The game operates NA1–8, EU1–8, AS1–3 (based on community reports; confirmed up to NA6 and EU6 via Steam news, with mention of up to NA8/EU8 in community discussions).

**Key principle**: Each server is completely self-contained:
- Separate leaderboards
- Separate guild rankings
- Separate economy (Auction House prices differ per server)
- Separate event timing (maintenance windows are server-group specific)
- Players CANNOT transfer between servers or access another server without creating a new character
- No cross-server play except for specially designated cross-server events

## 3. Server Maintenance Schedule

Kings Call follows a **regular weekly maintenance pattern** per server group:

**Maintenance Time**: Typically **Tuesdays, 4:00–6:00 PM Beijing time (UTC+8)**

- Maintenance is server-group-specific: EU1-4 may have maintenance at a different clock time than NA1-4 or AS1-3
- Announcements follow naming convention: "Announcement of Regularly Updated Maintenance (EU5) 20250325"
- The maintenance window is used to:
  1. Deploy new event content (rotating in new events, rotating out expired ones)
  2. Apply game balance patches
  3. Perform server infrastructure maintenance

**Documented 2025 Maintenance Announcements (EU5):**
- February 18, 2025
- March 11, 2025
- March 18, 2025
- March 25, 2025
- (And recurring weekly through 2025 per available data)

**Maintenance Impact on Players**:
- Servers are unavailable during the 2-hour window
- Players who are in battle when maintenance begins typically lose that session
- Reconnection bug: players who disconnect before maintenance and reconnect after sometimes lose rewards earned before disconnect

## 8. Guild System — Complete Documentation

### Creating and Joining Guilds

**To create a guild**: Standard guild creation interface; requires meeting a level or resource threshold (not precisely documented in available research)

**To join a guild**: Search by guild name in-game; guilds can set open or invitation-only membership

**Guild Capacity**: Each guild has a maximum member count; capacity increases as guild level increases

### Guild Levels and Economy

**Guild Experience (Guild EXP)**:
- Earned through: Player donations of Silver, guild quest completions, Guild Showdown participation
- As guild EXP accumulates, the guild levels up
- Higher guild levels unlock more Guild Skills and expand member capacity

**Player Donation System**:
- Players donate Silver from their own supply to the guild
- Each donation gives:
  1. Guild EXP (benefits everyone)
  2. Personal "Contribution Points" for the donating player
- Contribution Points can be spent in a guild shop (specific items not fully documented)

**Guild Skills**:
- Unlocked as the guild levels up
- 200+ guild skills documented in the Kings Call APK data
- Provide passive bonuses to all guild members across all game modes
- Examples of guild skill bonus types (from historical Rise of Mythos data, applicable here):
  - Cheaper card combination costs in Alchemy Lab
  - Higher success rates for card upgrades
  - Bonus experience from battles
  - Silver income bonuses
  - PvP stat increases for guild members
- Strategy tip: In active large guilds, the cumulative guild skill bonuses can be significant enough to make a meaningful difference in competitive play

**Guild Wealth**: The cumulative Silver donated tracks as "guild wealth" — a prestige metric and gate for higher-level guild facilities

### Guild Quests

- Available in-game as a quest type alongside personal/daily quests
- Completing guild quests earns Silver, guild EXP, and Reputation
- The more active the guild, the more guild quests are available
- **Do NOT cost Gold to complete** (key advantage over some Activity tasks)
- Recommended: Complete ALL guild quests every day before regular daily quests

### Guild Showdown (4v4 Tournament)

**Schedule**: Every Sunday (weekly)

**Format**:
- The top guilds on a server are matched against rival guilds in a bracket or round-robin format
- Within each matchup, **4 players from each guild** compete simultaneously
- Each player fights their individual opponent in their own lane-based match
- The team wins if they collectively win more matches than the opposing team

**Match Mechanics**:
- Standard card game combat rules apply
- Each player builds their own deck for the Showdown — these can be different decks optimized for different roles
- The battle is essentially simultaneous 1v1 across 4 parallel matches

**Coordination (Critical)**:
- Before the Showdown, team members must coordinate via guild chat or external Discord
- **Elite Card Overlap Rule**: Only one copy of any Elite card should be on the field at a time. If multiple players bring Princess Sarya, they waste potential synergy.
- Recommended team composition: 4 players with complementary roles:
  - Player 1: Heavy offense / glass cannon
  - Player 2: Control / Ranger
  - Player 3: Tank / sustain
  - Player 4: Balanced / counter pick

**Rewards**:
- Weekly guild contribution and personal contribution points
- Exclusive rewards for winning guilds (exact reward list not fully documented)
- "Massive prizes for their Team and the entire Guild" per official description

**Practice Mode**: Guild Showdown has a practice mode where you can run mock 4v4 matches with guild members without affecting rankings. Use this for coordination testing.

### Guild Ranking

- Server-wide guild leaderboard tracking guild strength/prestige
- Top guilds on each server are recognized in-game
- Known community-named guilds:
  - "Rise of Mythos" guild on EU1 (veteran player community)
  - "Loveliness" guild on EU2

## 9. PvP Arena System — Full Details

### Arena Modes

**Practice Modes (Unranked)**:
- 1v1 Practice: Challenge any specific player; no ranking impact; use for deck testing
- 2v2 Practice: Two-player partnership vs. two opponents; unranked
- 4v4 Practice: Four players per side; unranked; good for Guild Showdown preparation

**Ranked Modes**:
- 1v1 Ranked: Random matchmaking; affects personal 1v1 leaderboard score
- 2v2 Ranked: Partner-based; affects 2v2 leaderboard score

**Guild Showdown**:
- 4v4 guild competition; Sunday only; affects guild ranking
- (See Section 8 for full details)

### Matchmaking System

**Current Bot Range (as of March 2026 patch)**: 0–600 rating points
- Players in this range may face AI bots rather than human opponents
- This change was made to improve the experience for new/low-rated players who were struggling to find matches

**Above 600 rating**: Human vs. human matchmaking

### Ranking and Point System

- Points are won and lost based on match outcomes
- Rating differences affect how many points you gain/lose (winning against higher-rated opponents gives more points)
- Leaderboards tracked separately for 1v1 and 2v2

### Weekly Reset and Rewards

**Reset Time**: Tuesdays at 00:00 server time

**Requirement to Receive Weekly Rewards**: Complete minimum **50 total PvP battles** (any combination of 1v1, 2v2, 4v4)

**Weekly Reward Tiers** (base + leaderboard bonus):

| Rank | Gold | Medals | Bonus Items |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| Any (50 battles) | 20 | 5 | — (base reward) |
| Top 64 | 40 | 10 | — |
| Top 16 | 80 | 20 | — |
| Top 3 | 160 | 40 | Grey War Horse (cosmetic mount) |

### Medals of Honor (PvP Currency)

**Earning Medals**:
1. 50 PvP battles per week (base: 5 Medals)
2. Top 64 ranking: 10 Medals
3. Top 16 ranking: 20 Medals
4. Top 3 ranking: 40 Medals
5. Winning in daily/event PvP tournaments: varies
6. Top 200 on season-end ranking board: bonus Medals

**Spending Medals**:
- **PvP Shop**: Exchange Medals for specific cards that can only be obtained here
- Key cards in PvP Shop:
  - Princess Sarya: 1,500 Medals
  - Other season-exclusive cards (rotate each season)

### Season Structure

**Season Duration**: 4 weeks (28 days)

**End-of-Season Rewards** (based on documented Rise of Mythos/Kings Call data):
| Final Rank | Sealed League Tickets | Other Rewards |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------|
| Top 32 | 6 | 1× Gladiator Pack |
| 33–64 | 4 | 1× Gladiator Pack |

**VIP-Exclusive Post-Season Tournament**:
- The top **32 VIP players** per season (by spending) are invited to an exclusive post-season tournament
- F2P and low-VIP players are excluded regardless of skill
- Tournament rewards: Gold and exclusive cards
- This system is one of the most criticized aspects of Kings Call's monetization

## 10. World Boss Event (Queen Sharia)

**What It Is**: A server-wide PvE event where all players on a server collectively fight the same boss.

**Schedule**:
- Two battles per day
- Start times: 17:00 and 23:00 server time
- Duration per battle: 1 hour (or ends early when collective server damage = 450)

**Access Requirement**: Hero Level 20+

### Boss Mechanics

**Queen Sharia's Deck Options**: She randomly selects from 3 decks:
1. Angel deck
2. Demon deck
3. Dragon deck

**Boss State Progression** (based on time elapsed since battle start):
| State | Time Window | Difficulty |
|-------|------------|------------|
| Frail | 0:00 – 0:05 | Easiest — best time for max damage |
| Moderate | 0:05 – 0:15 | Normal difficulty |
| Reviving | 0:15 – 0:45 | Harder |
| Furious | 0:45 – end | Hardest |

**Victory Condition**: Total server collective damage reaches **450 damage**

### World Boss Rewards

| Condition | Reward |
|-----------|--------|
| Participated (at least 1 battle completed) | 50 Silver participation reward |
| Dealt 10+ damage | Reward box (Epic or Legendary card from featured Incarnate set) |
| Boss defeated by server | All reward boxes doubled |
| Maximum individual damage (server total ≥ 100) | Bonus "Max Damage" reward |

**Reward Box Contents**: Epic or Legendary cards from the current Incarnate set or same race as the featured set

**Strategy for Maximum Rewards**:
1. Enter during the Frail phase (first 5 minutes) when the boss has lowest difficulty
2. Use glass cannon burst decks (ranged + Mage) to maximize damage output in the Frail window
3. Even if you can't deal 10+ damage, participating for 1 complete battle still earns the 50 Silver participation reward

## 11. Challenge Hall (Cooperative Boss Fights)

**Format**: Up to 4 players team up against powerful boss enemies

**Available Scenarios**: 6+ different scenarios, each with race-specific enemies
- Each scenario emphasizes different tactics
- Scenarios unlock at different hero levels

**Difficulty Levels**:
- Casual (available for Shimmering Cave specifically)
- Normal
- Hard

**Battle Structure**:
- Each battle has 2 stages
- After Stage 1, Stage 2 begins
- Small chance in Stage 2 for an "Awakened" boss variation (much harder; uses Godlike/Awakened cards)
- Awakened boss versions provide significantly better rewards

**Reward System**:
- Experience and Silver from victories
- Reputation points (useful for Reputation Shop and stat bonuses)
- Card flips at end of each battle:
  - Soul Shards (crafting material)
  - Awakened Fragments (used for card awakening)
  - Special saddles/mounts (cosmetic)
  - Gold (30 Gold drop chance)

**Best Classes for Challenge Hall**:
- Mage: Best overall for boss killing (AoE damage and direct spell damage)
- Coordinate with team to avoid duplicate Elite cards (same advice as Guild Showdown)

## 12. Ascension Tower Server Structure

**Server Scope**: The Ascension Tower leaderboard is **server-specific** — you compete for rankings within your own server

**Weekly Reset**: Tower progress resets to Level 1 each week at the same time

**Cross-Server Implications**: Players who started on an older, more competitive server (like NA1, EU1) face harder Ascension Tower competition than players on newer servers. This is one reason veteran players sometimes recommend newer servers for progression.

## 13. Server Selection Strategy (Community Wisdom)

For new players deciding which server to join:

**New Servers (NA5+, EU5+, AS3+)**:
- Fresh economy — no established mega-rich players dominating the Auction House
- Guild competition is more balanced — everyone is starting together
- Ascension Tower and PvP rankings are achievable for free players without facing multi-year veterans
- New server gift codes provide a head start
- **Downside**: Smaller player pool initially; Guild Showdown may have fewer participants

**Older Servers (NA1, EU1, AS1)**:
- Established communities with veteran guilds
- NA1: Explicitly described as "whale central" by community — highest P2W concentration
- EU1: Home of "Rise of Mythos" guild — veteran community with deep game knowledge
- **Downside**: Very hard to compete in PvP or Ascension Tower as a new player against 1-2+ year veterans

**Recommendation from Community Consensus**: Start on the newest server available at time of joining. The "fresh start" advantage outweighs the smaller initial player pool.

## 14. H5 vs. Steam Server Separation — Technical Details

Confirmed by official FAQ (xstargame.com/news/index/details/id/73.html):

- H5 and Steam versions run on **completely separate servers**
- Data, progress, accounts, and purchases do NOT transfer between H5 and Steam
- Server naming may differ between H5 and Steam (unclear if they use the same NA1/EU1 naming)
- H5 has its own server infrastructure optimized for browser-based access

**Practical impact**:
- A player who spent $200 on Steam Kings Call cannot use those cards/progress on H5
- A player who farmed Epic cards on H5 for 3 months cannot use them on Steam
- Treat them as two completely separate games for progression purposes

**NA1 ("Whale Central")**:
- Described by community as the server with the highest concentration of heavy spenders
- PvP at high ranks is almost exclusively Awakened card vs. Awakened card battles
- F2P players report feeling completely uncompetitive in ranked play on this server
- Recommended: New F2P players should NOT start on NA1

**EU1 (Veterans' Server)**:
- Home of the "Rise of Mythos" guild — veteran players who have been playing since the original Rise of Mythos / Kings and Legends days
- Deep game knowledge community; helpful for new players learning the game
- More balanced than NA1 (fewer extreme whales) but still has experienced players with strong card collections

**EU2**:
- Home of the "Loveliness" guild
- Less documented in available research

**AS1 (Asia Server 1)**:
